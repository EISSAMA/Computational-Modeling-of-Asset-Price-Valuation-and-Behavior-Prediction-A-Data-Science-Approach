Computational Modeling of Asset Price Valuation and Behavior Prediction: A Data Science Approach is an interdisciplinary course that integrates Python programming, probability theory, computational science, and financial mathematics to study the dynamics of financial asset prices and their predictive modeling. The course begins with a practical introduction to Python programming for data analysis and scientific computing. Students are then introduced to the fundamental concepts of probability and statistical thinking that underpin modern quantitative finance. The course also provides an overview of the role of Data Science and Computational Science in financial modeling. Participants will explore different types of financial assets and gain a foundational understanding of how stock markets operate. The course then moves toward mathematical modeling of asset prices, starting with the classical Geometric Brownian Motion model. Students will learn how to collect historical stock data and estimate key model parameters such as the drift (μ) and volatility (σ). To support these models, the course introduces the theory of Stochastic Processes, including Brownian Motion as a fundamental building block for asset price dynamics. More advanced topics include stochastic models with jumps, which capture sudden market movements often observed in financial markets. The course also covers numerical methods for solving stochastic models and introduces techniques for volatility estimation and risk analysis. Finally, students will study the principles of option pricing and derivative valuation, including models based on stochastic dynamics such as the Black–Scholes model. Throughout the course, theoretical concepts are reinforced through Python-based simulations, real financial data analysis, and computational experiments, allowing students to build practical skills in quantitative finance and asset price modeling.
